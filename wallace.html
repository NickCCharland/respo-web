Wallace Falls State Park

Who Doesn't Love Waterfalls?

Located in Gold Bar, Washington, Wallace Falls State Park is definitely a gem. This 4,700 acre park has views that are bound to take your breath away. The scenery is outstanding in this park, which includes numerous waterfalls, three back-country lakes and a river. Wallace Falls drops from a height of 265 feet. The park offers a rock-climbing wall eight miles east near Index. Atop Mount Pilchuck, 60 miles away, a fire lookout provides a spectacular, panoramic view. Swimming access is five miles east at Big Eddy.

Oh, I forgot to mention that camping is also allowed in this park, so definitely be sure to take advantage of that.
